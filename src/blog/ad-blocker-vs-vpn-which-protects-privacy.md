# Ad Blocker vs VPN: Which One Actually Protects Your Privacy?

## Two Tools, Two Different Problems

VPNs and ad blockers operate at completely different layers of the internet stack and protect against completely different threats.

## What a VPN Does

A VPN encrypts your traffic and routes it through a VPN server, hiding your real IP from sites you visit and preventing your ISP from seeing which sites you access.

**Protects against:** ISP logging, network surveillance, IP visibility  
**Does NOT protect against:** Tracking cookies, analytics scripts, fingerprinting, retargeting pixels

A VPN user who visits a site with the Meta Pixel still has that visit reported to Meta — just from a different IP address.

## What AdShield Pro Does

AdShield Pro blocks advertising and tracking scripts at the browser application layer.

**Protects against:** Third-party trackers, ad pixels, fingerprinting scripts, retargeting, malvertising  
**Does NOT protect against:** IP visibility, ISP logging, network surveillance

## The Correct Mental Model

| Layer | Threat | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| Network | ISP sees all traffic | VPN |
| Network | Real IP visible to sites | VPN |
| Browser | Tracking scripts and pixels | AdShield Pro |
| Browser | Fingerprinting | AdShield Pro |
| Browser | Malvertising | AdShield Pro |

For most people, browser-level threats are the ones that directly result in surveillance advertising and data broker profiles. The ideal configuration: use both. AdShield Pro is free. A trustworthy VPN costs $5–10/month.
