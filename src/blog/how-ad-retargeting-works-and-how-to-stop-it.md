# How Ad Retargeting Works — And How to Stop It Permanently

## The Retargeting Mechanism

When you visit a product page, a retargeting pixel fires and records your visit in a custom audience associated with that brand's advertising account. The pixel captures a hashed version of your device fingerprint or cookie identifier.

## The Ad Auction

For the next 30–90 days, whenever you visit any site participating in Google's Display Network or Facebook's Audience Network, that brand bids in a real-time auction to show you an ad. Because you've been tagged as a high-intent visitor, they bid aggressively — and you see that shoe ad everywhere.

## Why It Feels Invasive

The ads follow you across completely unrelated sites — recipe sites, news readers, weather apps — because of a single tracking pixel fired when you browsed a product. This cross-site visibility is the advertising industry's most powerful and most unsettling capability.

## How to Stop It Permanently

AdShield Pro blocks retargeting pixels before they can fire and tag you:

- No pixel fire → no custom audience creation → no retargeting
- After 24–48 hours of AdShield Pro being active, the "following" ads disappear as existing audience memberships expire
- New visits never create new audience entries

The fix is permanent and automatic. Once the pixel cannot fire, the retargeting pipeline breaks at the source.
