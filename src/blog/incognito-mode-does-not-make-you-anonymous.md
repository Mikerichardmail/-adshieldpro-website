# Incognito Mode Does Not Make You Anonymous — Here Is the Truth

## The Incognito Myth

A 2021 study found that 56% of users believed incognito mode prevented websites from identifying them. This is incorrect — and the misunderstanding leaves millions of people with a false sense of privacy.

## What Incognito Mode Actually Does

Incognito prevents your browser from saving local data during that session:

- No browsing history saved to your device
- No cookies retained after the window closes
- No form data or autofill information saved

That is the complete list. Nothing else changes.

## What Incognito Does NOT Do

- Your IP address is still visible to every site you visit
- Your ISP still logs every DNS query and connection you make
- Your employer can still see your traffic on their network
- Browser fingerprinting still works identically — your hardware produces the same fingerprint in incognito
- Google still tracks you if you are signed into any Google service
- Tracking pixels still fire normally

## The Browser Fingerprint Problem

Your browser fingerprint is derived from hardware and software properties of your device — not from stored cookies. Clearing cookies changes nothing about your fingerprint. A tracker that has previously fingerprinted your device can re-identify you in incognito the moment you visit any participating site.

## When Incognito Is Actually Useful

- Borrowing someone else's computer without leaving traces
- Logging into a second account on the same service
- Seeing how a webpage appears to a logged-out visitor
