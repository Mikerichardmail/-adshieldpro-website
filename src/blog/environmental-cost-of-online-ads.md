# The Environmental Cost of Online Advertising — And How Blocking Helps

## How Much Energy Do Ads Use?

A 2022 study by Scope3 estimated that online advertising is responsible for approximately 60 million tonnes of CO2-equivalent emissions per year globally — roughly equivalent to the annual emissions of a mid-sized country. This includes:

- Data centre energy for ad servers, DSPs, SSPs, and exchanges
- CDN energy for delivering ad creative files globally
- End-device CPU energy for rendering and executing ad scripts

## The Per-Page Calculation

A typical ad-laden page loads 1–3 MB of ad content and runs 200–500 ms of JavaScript per page view. Across billions of daily page views globally, the cumulative energy consumption is significant.

On your individual device, blocking ads reduces CPU usage by 70–80% per page (less JavaScript to execute), which directly reduces power draw — measurably extending battery life and reducing the energy consumed per browsing session.

## The Bandwidth Dimension

Every blocked ad request is data that does not traverse the network. At the individual level this is kilobytes. Aggregated across millions of users blocking billions of ad requests daily, the network bandwidth savings are substantial.

Some network engineers have estimated that widespread ad blocking could reduce consumer internet backbone traffic by 15–25% — a significant infrastructure efficiency improvement.

## The Honest Framing

Individual action on digital environmental impact has limited scale compared to systemic changes. But blocking ads is one of the few individual digital choices with a real, immediate, measurable impact on energy consumption — alongside improving your browsing experience and privacy.
