# GDPR and CCPA: Why Cookie Consent Banners Do Not Actually Protect You

## The Promise and Reality of Privacy Law

GDPR and CCPA promised users meaningful control over their personal data. In practice, the consent banner ecosystem that emerged has often made things worse.

## What the Law Requires

**GDPR:** Websites must obtain freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent before setting non-essential cookies. Pre-ticked consent boxes are explicitly prohibited.

**CCPA:** Businesses must provide a "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" option and allow data deletion requests.

Meaningful requirements on paper.

## Dark Patterns in Consent Banners

The consent management platform (CMP) industry exists to maximise consent rates — not to help users make informed choices:

- "Accept All" in large green button, "Manage Preferences" in small grey text
- Reject option hidden behind 3–4 clicks
- Pre-selected boxes for all data uses (illegal under GDPR but widespread)
- Misleading language designed to confuse rather than inform

A 2020 study by the Norwegian Consumer Council found the majority of GDPR consent flows used at least one dark pattern, and that even explicit rejection often did not prevent cookies from being set.
