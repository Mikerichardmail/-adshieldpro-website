# Is Your Smart TV Spying on You? The Truth About ACR Technology

## What Is ACR?

Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) captures screenshots of your TV screen several times per second, identifies the content using audio and visual fingerprinting, and sends this data to the manufacturer's servers. It is built into virtually every smart TV sold since 2015.

## Which Brands Use ACR

- **Samsung** — "Viewing Information Services"
- **LG** — "Live Plus" / ThinQ AI platform
- **Vizio** — fined $2.2M by the FTC in 2017 for collecting ACR without adequate disclosure
- **Roku** — core of Roku's advertising business
- **Amazon Fire TV** — integrated with Amazon's advertising ecosystem

## How to Disable ACR on Your TV

**Samsung:** Settings → Support → Terms & Policies → disable "Viewing Information Services"

**LG:** Settings → All Settings → General → AI Service → disable "AI Recommendation" and "Live Plus"

**Vizio:** Menu → System → Reset & Admin → Smart Interactivity → Off

**Roku:** Settings → Privacy → Smart TV Experience → disable "Use Info from TV Inputs"

**Amazon Fire TV:** Settings → Preferences → Privacy Settings → disable "Collect App and Over-the-Air Usage Data"

## The Network-Level Solution

ACR relies on connecting to manufacturer servers. Blocking ACR endpoints at your DNS level (via Pi-hole or NextDNS on your router) prevents ACR from functioning even if a firmware update re-enables the setting — which some manufacturers have been known to do.
