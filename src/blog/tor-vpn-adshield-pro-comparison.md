# Tor vs VPN vs AdShield Pro: Which Privacy Tool Do You Actually Need?

## Three Tools, Three Different Threat Models

Tor, VPNs, and ad blockers are frequently conflated but operate at completely different levels and protect against different threats.

## Tor Browser

**Protects against:** IP tracking, network surveillance, traffic analysis, ISP logging, correlation attacks

**Does NOT protect against:** Browser fingerprinting within the session (though it standardises fingerprints across Tor users), JavaScript-based tracking, malvertising, application-layer tracking

**Trade-offs:** Significantly slower (traffic routes through three relays), some sites block Tor exit nodes, JavaScript-heavy sites may not work correctly

**Best for:** Journalists, activists, researchers, and anyone needing strong anonymity against well-resourced adversaries

## VPN

**Protects against:** ISP seeing your traffic, your real IP being visible to websites, network-level surveillance

**Does NOT protect against:** Cookies, fingerprinting, tracking pixels, anything at the browser application layer

**Trade-offs:** Requires trusting the VPN provider completely; speed varies; logs may exist

**Best for:** Hiding traffic from your ISP, accessing geo-restricted content, using untrusted networks (public WiFi, hotel)

## AdShield Pro

**Protects against:** Ad tracking, tracker scripts, fingerprinting, retargeting pixels, malvertising, cookie-based tracking, social media pixels

**Does NOT protect against:** IP visibility, ISP logging, network surveillance

**Trade-offs:** Occasionally causes site breakage (rare); does not help on non-browser apps

**Best for:** Everyday browsing privacy — stopping the surveillance advertising ecosystem from profiling you

## The Recommended Combination

For most people: **AdShield Pro + a trustworthy VPN** covers approximately 90% of real-world privacy threats encountered during normal browsing.

Add Tor for the highest-risk activities: investigative journalism, legal research on sensitive topics, or activism in high-surveillance environments.
