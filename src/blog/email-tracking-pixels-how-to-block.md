# How Email Tracking Pixels Work — And How to Block Them

## How Email Pixels Work

A tracking pixel is a 1×1 transparent image embedded in an HTML email. The image URL is unique to you and points to the sender's server. When your email client loads the image, the server logs your IP address, device type, email client, and the precise open time.

## What Senders Learn

- Exact time and date you opened the email
- Your approximate location via IP geolocation
- Your email client and operating system
- Whether you opened on mobile or desktop
- How many times you opened the email

## The Problem

This data scores you as an "engaged" contact for aggressive follow-up, feeds into CRM systems, and in some cases is used to prove you received a legal notice. In healthcare contexts, email pixels on appointment reminders have been used to infer patient conditions.

## How to Block Email Tracking

**Apple Mail (iOS 15+ / macOS Monterey+):** Mail Privacy Protection pre-fetches all email images through Apple's proxy, making pixel tracking unreliable. Enable at: Settings → Mail → Privacy Protection.

**Gmail:** Settings → See all settings → General → External images → "Ask before displaying external images"

**Outlook:** File → Options → Trust Center → Automatic Download → disable automatic image download.

**Browser-based email:** If you read email in a browser, AdShield Pro blocks the tracking pixel domains automatically — no additional configuration needed.
