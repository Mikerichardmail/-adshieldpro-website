# What's Actually In Your Google Advertising Profile?

## Viewing Your Google Ad Profile

Visit **myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy** and scroll to "Things you've done and places you've been." Under Ad Settings, click "Ad personalisation" to see Google's categorisation of your interests.

You will find categories ranging from broad (Sports, Technology, Travel) to surprisingly specific (Fertility & Pregnancy, Political Ideology, Mental Health, Specific Medical Conditions) depending on your browsing history.

## How Google Builds This Profile

Google aggregates signals from multiple sources:

- **Search queries** — every search you perform is stored and analysed
- **YouTube watch history** — videos watched, search terms, engagement patterns
- **Gmail content** — historically, though Google claims to have ended this practice
- **Map searches** — where you travel, businesses you search for
- **Cross-site tracking** — via Google Analytics (on 86% of major sites), Google Ad Manager, and DoubleClick
- **Android device data** — app usage, location history, and device identifiers if you use Android

## What You Can Do About It

**Delete your ad interest categories:** On the Ad personalisation page, you can remove individual interest categories. Google will stop using them for ad targeting — but may re-add categories based on future browsing.

**Turn off ad personalisation:** This prevents Google from using your data for personalised ads. Data is still collected.

**Delete your activity:** myaccount.google.com → Data & Privacy → My Activity. You can delete specific types of activity (search, YouTube, etc.) and set auto-delete periods (3 or 18 months).

**Use AdShield Pro:** Blocking Google Analytics, DoubleClick, and Google Ad Manager scripts prevents new data from being added to your profile during browsing sessions — the most reliable ongoing protection.
