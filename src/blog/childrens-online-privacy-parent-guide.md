# Children's Online Privacy in 2026: A Complete Parent's Guide

## The Law and Its Limits

COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) prohibits collecting personal data from children under 13 without parental consent in the US. In practice, enforcement is limited and many sites circumvent the requirement by claiming users must be 13+ without verification.

## What Actually Happens

Research consistently shows advertising trackers fire on sites clearly targeted at children — games, educational apps, and YouTube Kids channels. The tracking infrastructure does not distinguish between adult and child visitors.

## YouTube's Specific Problem

Despite a $170 million FTC fine in 2019 for improperly collecting data from children, data collected from children's YouTube viewing continues to be used in advertising contexts. AdShield Pro blocks YouTube's advertising and analytics scripts, reducing this data collection even on accounts marked as child accounts.

## Setting Up Protection for Children

**Browser level:**
- Install AdShield Pro on the family browser with Strict mode enabled
- Set up a separate Chrome profile for children
- Enable Safe Search and lock it via Google's Family Link

**Network level:**
- Configure NextDNS on your router with the Family Preset filter enabled — this blocks adult content and additional advertising networks at the DNS level for all devices on the network

**Device level:**
- Review app permissions on children's tablets and phones quarterly
- Enable Screen Time (iOS) or Digital Wellbeing (Android) parental controls
- Use a child-specific account on Android with Google Play Family controls

## The Practical Priority

Perfect protection is not achievable — but AdShield Pro at the browser level combined with NextDNS at the network level provides meaningful protection against the most common tracking vectors affecting children online.
