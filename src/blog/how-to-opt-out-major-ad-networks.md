# How to Opt Out of Every Major Advertising Network (And Why It Is Not Enough)

## The Opt-Out Illusion

Every major advertising network offers an opt-out mechanism. These tools are real but widely misunderstood — they stop targeted advertising, not data collection.

## Google Ad Settings

Visit **myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy → Ad Settings**. You can turn off ad personalisation across Google products. Google still receives your data across the web — it just stops using it to personalise the ads it shows you.

## Meta Ad Preferences

Facebook: **Settings → Ads → Ad Preferences → Ad Settings**. You can disable interest-based ads. The Meta Pixel still fires on every participating site. Facebook still builds your profile. Your data is still collected and may still be sold to data brokers.

## NAI and DAA Opt-Out

**optout.networkadvertising.org** allows you to opt out from approximately 100 ad networks simultaneously. Important limitations:
- These opt-outs work via cookies — clear your cookies and you must redo them
- They do not work on mobile apps
- New networks are not automatically covered

## AppChoices (Mobile)

The DAA's **AppChoices** app provides opt-outs for mobile advertising networks. Available on iOS and Android. Same limitation: opts out of targeting, not collection.

## The Fundamental Problem

Every opt-out mechanism described above operates at the **use** layer: companies agree not to use your data for personalised advertising. They continue to **collect** your data, **store** it, and potentially **sell** it to data brokers.

Blocking at the source (AdShield Pro) prevents collection entirely — which is meaningfully more protective than any opt-out mechanism.
