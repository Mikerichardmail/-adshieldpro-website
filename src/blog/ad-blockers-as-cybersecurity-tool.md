# Why Ad Blockers Are Now a Cybersecurity Tool, Not Just a Convenience

## What Is Malvertising?

Malvertising is the delivery of malware through advertising networks. An attacker purchases ad inventory through a legitimate programmatic platform and serves an ad that exploits browser vulnerabilities or redirects users to phishing pages.

## Recent High-Profile Cases

In 2024–2025, malvertising campaigns targeted Google search ads — redirecting users searching for legitimate software (Zoom, Slack, WinRAR) to convincing fake download pages that served malware. These ads appeared above the legitimate sites in Google's own search results.

## Why Ad Networks Cannot Fully Prevent It

The programmatic advertising ecosystem processes billions of ad impressions daily through automated systems. Manual review is impossible at scale. Sophisticated attackers use delayed payload activation to pass initial screening before switching to malicious delivery.

## Ad Blocking as Defence in Depth

No ad means no malvertising delivery vector. Blocking ads entirely is one of the most reliable defences available — more reliable than hoping ad networks catch every malicious campaign before it reaches users.

Many enterprise security teams now mandate ad blockers for exactly this reason. The FBI and CISA have both issued advisories recommending ad blockers as a protection against malvertising campaigns.
