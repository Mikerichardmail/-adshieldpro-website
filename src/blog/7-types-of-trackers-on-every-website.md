# The 7 Types of Trackers Hidden on Every Website You Visit

## The Invisible Surveillance Layer of the Web

A typical top-100 website contains between 8 and 25 distinct tracking technologies. Most users know about cookies — but cookies are just one of many mechanisms advertisers use to follow you across the internet.

## 1. Analytics Trackers

Tools like Google Analytics record every page view, click, scroll depth, and time on page. Sessions are linked across pages, and data often flows to advertising platforms for targeting.

## 2. Advertising and Retargeting Trackers

Ad network trackers drop cookies when you view a product. For the next 30–90 days they bid on ad inventory to show follow-up ads across millions of websites.

## 3. Session Replay Scripts

Hotjar, FullStory, and Microsoft Clarity literally record your screen: every mouse movement, scroll, click, and keystroke — including text entered into form fields before submission.

## 4. Social Media Pixels

The Meta Pixel and TikTok Pixel fire on millions of third-party websites, reporting your visits back to those platforms whether you are logged in or not.

## 5. CNAME Cloaking

Third-party trackers disguise themselves as first-party subdomains to evade blockers. AdShield Pro uses DNS-level analysis to detect and block these.

## 6. URL Tracking Parameters

Parameters like `?fbclid=` and `?gclid=` allow platforms to link your identity across sites. AdShield Pro strips these automatically.

## 7. Browser Storage Supercookies

Beyond standard cookies, trackers use IndexedDB, localStorage, and HSTS headers to store persistent identifiers that survive cookie deletion. Blocking the scripts before they run is the only reliable defence.
