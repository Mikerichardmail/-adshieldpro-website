# The Death of the Third-Party Cookie: What Replaces It?

## The End of Third-Party Cookies

Google began deprecating third-party cookies in Chrome in 2024, completing the process through 2025. Third-party cookies were the primary cross-site tracking mechanism for over 25 years. Their removal was a significant moment — but it was not the end of tracking.

## The Privacy Sandbox Replacements

Google's Privacy Sandbox introduced replacement APIs:

**Topics API:** The browser itself categorises your interests based on your browsing history and shares these categories with advertisers directly. Rather than trackers building your profile externally, your browser becomes the profiling mechanism.

**Protected Audience API (FLEDGE):** Ad auctions happen inside the browser rather than on external servers. Your remarketing audiences are stored locally, but the auction result still serves a targeted ad.

## Why Critics Say This Is Worse

With third-party cookies, tracking happened on external servers that could be blocked by DNS or extension rules. With the Topics API, the tracking logic runs inside Chrome itself. Opting out requires using a different browser entirely — Google controls both the browser and the "privacy" mechanism.
