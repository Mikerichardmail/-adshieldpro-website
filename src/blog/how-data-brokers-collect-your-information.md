# How Data Brokers Collect Your Personal Information — The Full Picture

## The Data Broker Industry

There are an estimated 4,000 data broker companies globally. The largest — Acxiom, Oracle Data Cloud, LexisNexis — hold records on hundreds of millions of people including home addresses, purchase histories, political affiliations, health interests, and behavioural profiles.

## Where the Data Comes From

**Retail loyalty programs:** Every supermarket and pharmacy loyalty card purchase is logged against your identity and sold to brokers.

**Mobile app permissions:** Thousands of free apps monetise by selling location data and behavioural signals. A weather app with location permission reports your GPS coordinates every few minutes.

**Public records:** Property ownership, voter registrations, court filings, marriage records — all systematically harvested and linked.

**Online tracking:** The web tracker ecosystem feeds data brokers directly. Blocking third-party trackers with AdShield Pro reduces the real-time data feed into broker databases.

**Data purchases from other companies:** Brokers buy from each other, from retailers, from financial services, and from platform operators.

## The Opt-Out Problem

Most brokers offer opt-outs, but each requires a separate process, opt-outs expire, and new brokers enter the market continuously. Services like DeleteMe and Kanary automate opt-outs across hundreds of brokers.
